Nagisa Ōshima

One of the foremost directors within the Japanese New Wave

A time line of Nagisa Ōshima's life:

  • 1932 - Nagisa Ōshima was born in Kyoto, Japan.
  • 1954 - Ōshima graduated from Kyoto University with a degree in political science
  • 1959 - Ōshima directed his first feature film, "A Town of Love and Hope," which was a critical and commercial success.
  • 1960 - Ōshima directed "Night and Fog in Japan," which caused controversy for its political content and was temporarily banned by Japanese authorities.
  • 1961 - Ōshima joined the Japanese Communist Party, which influenced his filmmaking.
  • 1964 - Ōshima directed "Empire of Passion," which won the Best Director award at the Cannes Film Festival.
  • 1968 - Ōshima directed "Death by Hanging," a film that explored issues of capital punishment and identity.
  • 1976 - Ōshima directed "In the Realm of the Senses," a sexually explicit film based on a true story that was banned in several countries.
  • 1983 - Ōshima directed "Merry Christmas Mr. Lawrence," which starred David Bowie and was a critical and commercial success.
  • 1986 - Ōshima served as the head of the jury at the Cannes Film Festival.
  • 1989 - Ōshima directed "Taboo," a film that explored homosexuality in feudal Japan.
  • 1990 - Ōshima received the Japanese government's Order of Culture for his contributions to film.
  • 1992 - Ōshima was diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease, which caused him to retire from filmmaking.
  • 1999 - Ōshima suffered a stroke that left him partially paralyzed and unable to continue making films.
  • 2003 - A retrospective of Ōshima's films was held at the Lincoln Center in New York City.
  • 2008 - Ōshima's final film, "Gohatto," was released.
  • 2010 - Ōshima was awarded the Legion of Honor by the French government for his contributions to cinema.
  • 2012 - Ōshima's health deteriorated, and he was moved to a nursing home.
  • 2013 - Nagisa Ōshima passed away at the age of 80 due to pneumonia.
